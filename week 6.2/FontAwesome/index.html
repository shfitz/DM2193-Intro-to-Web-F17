Font Awesome

Font Awesome is a collection of 600+ icons and pictographs tht are free to use. It's primarily placed in your site in one of two ways. Either place directly in your own site/directory, or use their CDN.

There's a massive list of available icons, and it's pretty straightforward to drop them into your page.

It's designed to be used with any inline element. And while semantically you should use the <span> element, Font Awesome usues <i> in their examples because "it's three less letters to type", which means we can all be a little lazier today.

In use, it's really simple. You add a class to your inline element, like so : <i class="fa fa-paper-plane">. This will give us the paper plane icon : . It will inheret things like color and size too. For example, <i class="fa fa-paper-plane" style="color: blueviolet; font-size: 32px"> will give you this : .

You can increase sizes relative to the container with the classes fa-lg and fa-2x thru fa-5x.


It's possible to do fancy things like these near quotes too. Here, classes are added for moving the icon to the left and adding a border around the icon itself.

And, what fun would an icon be if it can't be distracting as possible? The spin class animates icons, while the pulse class does an 8-step animation, whch works well with some of their loading icons :
<i class="fa fa-cog fa-spin fa-3x" aria-hidden="true">
<i class="fa fa-spinner fa-pulse fa-3x" aria-hidden="true">
